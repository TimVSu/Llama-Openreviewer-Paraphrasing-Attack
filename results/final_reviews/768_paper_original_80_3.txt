### Summary
This paper proposes a method for detecting lexical entailment in context. The method consists of a few different components: (1) contextualized word representations, (2) context representations, and (3) similarity features. The authors evaluate their method on three datasets: Context-PPDB, Context-WN, and a cross-lingual dataset. The results show that the proposed method outperforms context-agnostic baselines and establishes a new state-of-the-art on an existing dataset.

## Strengths
- The paper is well-written and easy to follow.
- The proposed method is simple and effective, and the authors provide a clear explanation of each component.
- The evaluation is thorough, with experiments on three datasets and comparisons to multiple baselines.

## Weaknesses
- The paper could benefit from more ablation studies to analyze the contribution of each component.
- The paper does not provide a detailed analysis of the cross-lingual dataset, which could be interesting given the cross-lingual setting.
